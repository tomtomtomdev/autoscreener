## Key Howard Marks Quotes by Theme

### On Risk
> *"Risk means more things can happen than will happen."*
> *"Risk is not knowing what you're doing."*
> *"The riskiest thing in the world is the belief that there's no risk."*

### On Cycles
> *"The market is a pendulum that forever swings between unsustainable optimism and unjustified pessimism."*
> *"We can't know where we're going, but we should know where we are."*

### On Second-Level Thinking
> *"To outperform the average investor, you have to be able to outthink the consensus. Are you capable of doing so? What makes you think so?"*

### On Contrarianism
> *"Resisting — and avoiding the consequences of — the emotional extremes is one of the most important things a superior investor can do."*

### On Humility
> *"The biggest investing errors come not from factors that are informational or analytical, but from those that are psychological."*

### On Defense
> *"The secret of investing is that there is no secret. To be a superior investor, you have to know something that others don't, or process the same information better, or be more disciplined."*

## The Illuminated Edition — Key Additional Commentary

The Illuminated Edition (2013) adds margin commentary from four investors.
Brief summary of each commentator's lens:

**Joel Greenblatt** (Gotham Capital — Magic Formula)
- Adds quantitative screens for value: ROIC + earnings yield
- Reinforces that cheap + quality = best combination
- Relevant when looking for IDX screening criteria

**Seth Klarman** (Baupost — distressed/deep value)
- Adds emphasis on *margin of safety* — buy far below intrinsic value
- Warns against using low interest rates to justify high P/E (a warning that aged well)
- Relevant for bond and distressed positioning

**Paul Johnson** (Columbia Business School)
- Adds academic framing: behavioral finance underpinning for Marks' intuitions
- Links to Kahneman, Thaler, Shiller's work
- Relevant for understanding *why* psychology biases persist

**Christopher Davis** (Davis Advisors — long-term quality)
- Adds focus on business quality as protective moat
- Emphasizes owner-operator alignment
- Relevant for holding period decisions

## The Marks/Graham-Dodd Integration Map

How to use both skills together:

```
Step 1 (Graham-Dodd):   Estimate intrinsic value of an asset
Step 2 (Marks):         Assess where we are in the cycle
Step 3 (Marks):         Assess current sentiment (fear vs. greed)
Step 4 (Marks):         Determine aggressiveness of positioning
Step 5 (Marks):         Perform second-level thinking check
Step 6 (Graham-Dodd):   Verify margin of safety is sufficient
Step 7 (Marks):         Execute with appropriate position sizing
```
