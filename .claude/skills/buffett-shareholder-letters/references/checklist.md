# Analysis Checklist, Scorecard & Decision Tree

Use this to turn the framework into a verdict. Work top to bottom; the early gates can end
the analysis before you reach valuation.

## Stage gates (answer in order)

### Gate 1 — Circle of competence
- Can I explain in plain language how this business makes money?
- Can I form a reasonable view of what it looks like in 10 years?
- **If either answer is no → PASS now.** "Too hard" is a valid, correct verdict.

### Gate 2 — Franchise test (the moat)
- Is the product/service needed or desired?
- Do customers believe there is no close substitute?
- Is it free of binding price regulation?
- What happens if a well-funded, talented competitor attacks? Does the moat hold?
- Evidence: high pretax return on net tangible assets; pricing power; stable/growing share.
- **If no durable franchise → there is no long-term-hold thesis.** It may still be a cheap
  cigar-butt, but treat it as that, not as a compounder.

### Gate 3 — Owner earnings & returns on capital
- Compute owner earnings (separate maintenance from growth capex; show the range).
- Compute pretax return on net tangible assets — is it well above market rates?
- Does economic goodwill appear to be growing, and does it hold up in inflation?

### Gate 4 — Capital allocation & management
- One-dollar test over rolling ~5 years: ≥ $1 of market value per $1 retained?
- Are buybacks made below intrinsic value, dividends paid when retention fails the test?
- Is reporting candid? Are mistakes owned?
- Any signs of the institutional imperative (empire-building, fund-soaking M&A, herd
  behavior, leader's whims rubber-stamped)?

### Gate 5 — Intrinsic value & margin of safety
- Discount owner earnings (two-stage), anchor `r` to the long bond, keep `g` conservative.
- Produce a low/base/high intrinsic-value range, not a point.
- Compute the implied margin of safety at the current price.
- Does the verdict survive plausible assumption changes? If it flips easily, downgrade
  confidence (effectively a circle-of-competence failure on valuation).

### Gate 6 — Risk (Buffett's definition)
- Certainty of long-term economics?
- Certainty of management's ability and shareholder-orientation?
- Is the price right?
- What do taxes and inflation do to the real return?

## Scorecard

Score each dimension 0–5 (0 = clearly fails, 5 = exemplary). The weights reflect the
letters' emphasis on business quality over cheapness.

| Dimension                          | Weight | Score (0–5) | Weighted |
|------------------------------------|:------:|:-----------:|:--------:|
| Within circle of competence        |  15%   |             |          |
| Franchise / moat durability        |  25%   |             |          |
| Returns on tangible capital        |  15%   |             |          |
| Owner-earnings quality & growth    |  10%   |             |          |
| Capital allocation & management    |  20%   |             |          |
| Margin of safety at current price  |  15%   |             |          |
| **Total**                          | 100%   |             |          |

Interpretation (guideline, not a rule — the gates above can override):
- **Weighted ≥ 4.0 and all gates passed** → strong candidate.
- **3.0–4.0** → watch-list; revisit on a better price or clearer moat evidence.
- **< 3.0, or any hard gate failed** → pass.

A failed Gate 1 (competence) or Gate 2 (no moat, for a hold thesis) overrides a high score
elsewhere. Quality first, price second.

## Decision tree

```
Understand the business?
  │
  ├─ No ─────────────────────────────────────────────► PASS ("too hard")
  │
  └─ Yes
       │
       ▼
  Durable economic franchise / moat?
       │
       ├─ No ──► Only a cheap-asset (cigar-butt) case? 
       │           ├─ No ──────────────────────────────► PASS
       │           └─ Yes ─► treat as special-situation, NOT a compounder; small size
       │
       └─ Yes
            │
            ▼
       High returns on tangible capital + good capital allocation?
            │
            ├─ No ───────────────────────────────────► PASS or WATCH (governance risk)
            │
            └─ Yes
                 │
                 ▼
            Price below intrinsic-value range with adequate margin of safety?
                 │
                 ├─ No ──► WATCH-LIST (right business, wrong price — wait for Mr. Market)
                 │
                 └─ Yes ─► BUY, sized to conviction; intend to hold while the moat endures
```

## Reporting template

Deliver the verdict in this shape:

```
# [Company] — Buffett/Berkshire-lens assessment

## Circle of competence
[Can we understand it? 10-year visibility? If no, stop here.]

## Franchise & moat
[Franchise vs. business verdict + the reason. Moat sources and the competitor stress-test.]

## Owner earnings & returns on capital
[Owner-earnings range with the maintenance-capex assumption stated.
 Pretax return on net tangible assets. Economic-goodwill / inflation note.]

## Capital allocation & management
[One-dollar test result. Buyback/dividend discipline. Candor. Institutional-imperative flags.]

## Intrinsic value & margin of safety
[Low/base/high intrinsic-value range, key assumptions (r, g), implied margin of safety now.]

## Risk
[Buffett's four factors. What would break the thesis.]

## Verdict
[BUY / WATCH / PASS, with the two or three swing factors that would change it.]
```

Keep every estimate honest: state assumptions, prefer ranges, and never dress a judgment up
as a fact.
