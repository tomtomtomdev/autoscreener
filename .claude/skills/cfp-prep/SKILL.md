# CFP Preparation

A mentor's roadmap for the CFP (Certified Financial Planner) — the global standard for the *client-advising / financial-planning* path. This is distinct from the CFA: CFP is about advising people holistically on their money (cash flow, insurance, tax, retirement, estate, investments), whereas the CFA is about analyzing securities and managing portfolios. Choose CFP if the goal is to plan for and advise clients.

## Verify before relying on specifics

CFP standards are administered by the **Financial Planning Standards Board (FPSB)** globally, by the **CFP Board** in the United States, and locally by national affiliates such as **FPSB Indonesia**. Requirements, tiered local certifications, exam formats, and fees vary by country and change over time. Treat the structure below as a map — **confirm current requirements with the relevant local body** (in Indonesia, FPSB Indonesia) before committing.

## What it is and who it's for

A practitioner credential for people who advise individuals and households on comprehensive financial planning. Where the CFA goes deep on valuation and portfolio theory, the CFP goes broad across the planning disciplines a client actually needs. Note that advising clients for compensation is frequently a *regulated* activity — in Indonesia, professional investment-management and brokerage roles additionally require OJK licenses (see the `wmi-prep` and `wppe-prep` skills); the CFP is a competency standard layered on top of, not a substitute for, any required local license.

## The "four E's" (the universal requirements)

1. **Education** — complete an FPSB-approved education program covering the planning curriculum.
2. **Examination** — pass the CFP certification exam (scenario- and case-based, testing integrated planning, not just recall).
3. **Experience** — accumulate qualifying financial-planning work experience (the required hours/years vary by jurisdiction).
4. **Ethics** — agree to and uphold the code of ethics and standards of conduct, including a fiduciary-style duty to act in the client's interest.

Some affiliates (including FPSB Indonesia) offer **tiered or stepping-stone certifications** that build toward the full CFP — verify the current local ladder.

## The planning domains tested

The CFP body of knowledge spans the full personal-finance picture:

- General principles of financial planning and the planning process
- Risk management and insurance
- Investment planning
- Income tax planning
- Retirement planning and employee benefits
- Estate planning
- Professional conduct and regulation / ethics

The defining skill is **integration** — the exam rewards weaving these domains into a single coherent plan for a client scenario, including trade-offs between them.

## How to prepare (sequencing)

1. **Take the approved education program first** — unlike the CFA, self-study from third-party books alone usually won't satisfy the education requirement.
2. **Learn the planning *process*, not just facts.** Practice building end-to-end plans for case scenarios.
3. **Drill tax, retirement, and estate** — these are technical and jurisdiction-specific, and trip up candidates from an investments-only background.
4. **Practice case studies and mock exams** in the final stretch, treating each as a client you must actually advise.

## Reputable materials

- **FPSB-approved education providers** in your country (in Indonesia, those endorsed by FPSB Indonesia) — these are required, not optional.
- Official FPSB / local-affiliate study materials and practice cases.
- Be skeptical of any provider not recognized by the local affiliate, and of anyone selling the credential as a fast track to "selling products."
