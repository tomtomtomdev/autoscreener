# WMI Preparation (Wakil Manajer Investasi)

A mentor's roadmap for the **WMI — Wakil Manajer Investasi** (Investment Manager Representative), the Indonesian license required to work as a representative of an investment manager (managing portfolios and mutual funds / reksa dana). This is the local *regulatory* license for the fund-management path; it is occupation-specific and Indonesia-specific, and complements the analytical skill that a CFA or the `investing-curriculum` skill builds.

## Verify before relying on specifics

The Indonesian capital-market licensing framework is set by **OJK (Otoritas Jasa Keuangan)**, with professional certification exams administered through the **Panitia Standar Profesi** framework and prepared/run by **TICMI (The Indonesia Capital Market Institute)**, part of the IDX group. Exam modules, fees, validity periods, and continuing-education (PPL) requirements change. Treat the structure below as a map — **confirm current requirements with TICMI and OJK** before committing.

## What it is and who it's for

WMI is for people who want to **manage investment portfolios and funds professionally** in Indonesia — working at or as a representative of an investment manager (Manajer Investasi). If the goal is brokerage/securities dealing rather than fund management, the relevant license is WPPE instead (see `wppe-prep`); for underwriting it is WPEE.

## The path (certification then license)

1. **Sit the WMI certification exam** through TICMI / the Panitia Standar Profesi. The exam is typically delivered in **multiple modules/papers** that must each be passed (verify the current module breakdown and any sequencing).
2. **Obtain the certification**, then **apply to OJK for the individual license** (izin orang perseorangan) to act as WMI.
3. **Maintain the license** through any required continuing professional education (PPL) and renewal — confirm current cadence.

Preparation courses offered by TICMI are the standard route and align directly with the exam.

## Exam subject areas (typical — verify current syllabus)

The WMI body of knowledge centers on investment management within the Indonesian regulatory context:

- **Capital-market law and regulation** — the Undang-Undang Pasar Modal and OJK regulations governing investment managers and funds.
- **Code of ethics and professional conduct** — fiduciary duties, conflicts of interest.
- **Economics and financial markets** — macro and market fundamentals.
- **Financial and company analysis** — reading statements and valuing securities.
- **Portfolio and investment management** — modern portfolio theory, asset allocation, performance measurement.
- **Mutual funds (reksa dana) and investment products** — structures, regulation, and operations.

The regulation and ethics content is Indonesia-specific and is where internationally trained candidates most often need focused study.

## How to prepare (sequencing)

1. **Take the TICMI preparation course** — it maps to the exam modules and the current syllabus.
2. **Front-load regulation and ethics** — these are the most jurisdiction-specific and heavily tested portions, and the hardest to absorb from general finance reading.
3. **Lean on existing analytical skill** for the analysis/portfolio modules if you already have a CFA or value-investing background — that content will be partly review.
4. **Practice past-style questions per module** and confirm you can clear each module's threshold.

## How this connects to the broader study

The analysis and portfolio-management content overlaps with Phases 0–5 of the `investing-curriculum` skill and with CFA Levels I–II — so analytical preparation transfers. What WMI adds, and what no international credential substitutes for, is **the Indonesian legal/regulatory layer and the OJK license itself**, which is what legally permits professional fund management in Indonesia. A common combination is CFA (or the curriculum) for analytical depth plus WMI for the local license.
