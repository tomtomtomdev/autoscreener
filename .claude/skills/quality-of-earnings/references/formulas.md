# Formulas & Thresholds — Quick Reference

All calculations the skill uses, with interpretation bands. Thresholds are guidelines, not bright lines — always read direction and persistence over multiple periods, and contextualize by industry. Bands below assume a "normal" non-financial company; adjust for sector norms.

## Core / adjusted earnings

**Core operating earnings** = Reported net income − after-tax nonrecurring & nonoperating items
(asset-sale gains, settlements, debt-retirement gains, one-time tax benefits, pension income, accounting-change gains, reserve releases)

**Adjusted ("quality") EPS** = Core operating earnings, further reversing non-economic accounting boosts you can quantify (e.g., the EPS effect of a useful-life extension, a LIFO liquidation, an unsustainable tax-rate drop) ÷ diluted shares

> Show every adjustment as a line item. Label it an estimate. The point is to see *underlying* earning power and how far it sits below the headline.

**Earnings-overstatement gap** = (Reported EPS − Adjusted EPS) / Reported EPS
- < 5% → headline is trustworthy
- 5–15% → modest dependence on soft items
- > 15% → headline materially overstates sustainable earning power

## Lens 2 — Receivables

**AR growth %** = (AR_t − AR_{t-1}) / AR_{t-1}
**Sales growth %** = (Sales_t − Sales_{t-1}) / Sales_{t-1}
**AR–sales gap** = AR growth % − Sales growth %
- ≤ 0 → 🟢
- 0 to +10 pts for one period → 🟡
- > +10 pts, or any positive gap sustained 2–3+ periods → 🔴

**Days sales outstanding (DSO)** = (Accounts receivable / Sales) × period days
- Flat/declining → 🟢; rising trend → 🟡; sharp jump or steady multi-year climb → 🔴

**Allowance ratio** = Allowance for doubtful accounts / Gross receivables
- Falling while DSO rises → quiet earnings boost, 🟡/🔴

## Lens 3 — Inventory

**Inventory growth % vs. sales (or COGS) growth %** — same gap logic and bands as AR.
**Inventory turnover** = COGS / Average inventory  → falling = 🟡/🔴
**Days inventory** = (Inventory / COGS) × period days → rising = 🟡/🔴
**Finished-goods share** = Finished goods / Total inventory → rising share is the strongest warning.
**LIFO liquidation effect** — pulled from footnote; subtract the disclosed pre-tax benefit from gross profit before trending.

## Lens 4 — Depreciation

**D&A / Capex** = D&A / Capital expenditures → trend it; D&A falling while assets grow warrants scrutiny.
**Depreciation rate** = Depreciation expense / Average gross PP&E → a falling rate without a mix change can signal life-extension; cross-check the footnote.
**EPS effect of a method/life change** = disclosed pre-tax expense reduction × (1 − tax rate) ÷ diluted shares → treat as low-quality earnings.

## Lens 5 — Discretionary costs

**R&D / Sales, Advertising / Sales, Maintenance (or SG&A) / Sales** — trend each.
- Stable or rising into investment → 🟢 (quality may exceed headline)
- Sudden contraction coinciding with an earnings "beat" → 🔴 (earnings borrowed from the future)

## Lens 6 — Taxes

**Effective tax rate** = Income tax expense / Pretax income
- Compare to statutory rate; a *declining* effective rate flatters EPS growth.

**Tax-normalized EPS** = Pretax income × (1 − sustainable tax rate) ÷ diluted shares → compare growth vs. reported to isolate operations-driven growth.

**Deferred tax liability trend** — rising DTL = book income outrunning taxable income = more aggressive book accounting. Persistent rise → 🟡/🔴.

## Lens 7 — Cash flow (weight this heavily)

**OCF / Net income** (cash conversion)
- ≥ 1.0 sustained → 🟢
- 0.8–1.0 → 🟡
- < 0.8, or declining while net income rises → 🔴

**Accrual ratio** = (Net income − Operating cash flow) / Average total assets
- Low/negative → 🟢 (cash-backed)
- High positive, or rising → 🔴 (earnings ahead of cash; historically linked to weaker forward returns)

**Free cash flow** = OCF − Capex → earnings that don't become FCF over a full cycle are suspect.
**FCF conversion** = FCF / Net income → trend it across the cycle.

## Lens 8 — Hidden liabilities

**Pension expected return on assets / discount rate** — compare to peers and to plausible market returns; outlier-high assumptions inflate earnings.
**Capitalized costs / Total costs** — rising = more expense deferred off the income statement; flag policy changes.
**Reserve balance trend & releases** — releases into weak quarters = smoothing.
**Off-balance-sheet:** operating-lease commitments, guarantees, purchase obligations — restate as debt-equivalents when material to judge true leverage.

## Composite quality read

Score each of the eight lenses 🟢=2 / 🟡=1 / 🔴=0, **double-weighting Lens 7 (cash flow) and the worse of Lenses 2/3**. Then map total to a rating — but let the narrative override the arithmetic when one severe red flag (e.g., net income up, OCF down three years running) dominates:

- **High quality** — earnings are conservative, cash-backed, sustainable; headline ≈ adjusted.
- **Medium quality** — some reliance on soft items or stretched assumptions; headline modestly overstates.
- **Low quality** — earnings lean on one-time items, aggressive assumptions, and/or diverge from cash; headline materially overstates sustainable earning power.
