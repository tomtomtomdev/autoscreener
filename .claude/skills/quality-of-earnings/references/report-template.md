# Report Template & Checklist

Use this structure for the earnings-quality write-up. Keep it tight: a reader should see the verdict, the adjusted number, the scorecard, and the two-to-four flags that actually matter — without wading through every ratio.

## Pre-flight checklist

Before writing, confirm you have run (or explicitly noted you couldn't run) each lens:

- [ ] **L1 Nonrecurring/nonoperating** — identified and stripped one-time items
- [ ] **L2 Receivables** — AR-vs-sales gap and DSO trend
- [ ] **L3 Inventory** — inventory-vs-sales, turnover, finished-goods mix, LIFO/FIFO
- [ ] **L4 Depreciation** — method/life changes, D&A vs. capex
- [ ] **L5 Discretionary costs** — R&D / advertising / maintenance as % of sales
- [ ] **L6 Taxes** — effective vs. statutory rate, one-time benefits, deferred-tax trend
- [ ] **L7 Cash flow** — OCF/NI, accrual ratio, FCF (weight heavily)
- [ ] **L8 Hidden liabilities** — pension assumptions, capitalization, reserves, off-balance-sheet, letter read
- [ ] Computed **adjusted EPS** and the **overstatement gap**
- [ ] Listed **missing data / unverified flags**

## Report structure

ALWAYS use this template:

```
# Earnings-Quality Assessment — [Company], [Period(s)]

## Verdict: [High / Medium / Low] quality
One-paragraph synthesis: what the headline says, what the company is really
earning, and the dominant reason for the rating.

## Headline vs. adjusted
- Reported EPS / net income: [value]
- Adjusted ("core") EPS: [value]
- Overstatement gap: [%]
- Adjustments (each a line, marked as estimates):
  - [item] → [+/- EPS effect]

## Eight-lens scorecard
| Lens | Signal | One-line reason |
|------|--------|-----------------|
| 1 Nonrecurring income      | 🟢/🟡/🔴 | … |
| 2 Receivables vs. sales    | 🟢/🟡/🔴 | … |
| 3 Inventory vs. sales      | 🟢/🟡/🔴 | … |
| 4 Depreciation             | 🟢/🟡/🔴 | … |
| 5 Discretionary costs      | 🟢/🟡/🔴 | … |
| 6 Taxes                    | 🟢/🟡/🔴 | … |
| 7 Cash flow vs. earnings   | 🟢/🟡/🔴 | … |
| 8 Hidden liabilities       | 🟢/🟡/🔴 | … |

## What matters most
The 2–4 flags that drive the verdict, explained — each with the number, the
trend, the footnote/disclosure it rests on, and the benign explanation you
considered and why you did or didn't rule it out.

## Caveats & missing data
- What you couldn't verify (e.g., footnotes not provided, single-period only)
- Which flags are unconfirmed as a result

## Not advice
This assesses earnings quality and accounting risk only. It is not a
recommendation to buy, sell, or hold. Combine it with valuation and your own
judgment.
```

## Tone and rigor reminders

- **Lead with the verdict and the adjusted number** — that's what the reader came for.
- **Cite the disclosure** behind any accounting flag; if you don't have it, say the flag is unconfirmed rather than asserting it.
- **Always pair a red flag with the benign alternative** you considered. Forensic skepticism is not the same as assuming guilt — O'Glove's edge was patient triangulation, not paranoia.
- **Weight cash flow and working-capital signals most.** Accruals can be managed; multi-year cash divergence is the hardest thing to fake.
- **Trend over level.** Note whether each flag is a one-period blip or a multi-year pattern; persistence is what turns 🟡 into 🔴.
